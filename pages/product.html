The Pacific Blue Tang (Paracanthurus hepatus), famously known as Dory, is a vibrant and popular marine fish native to coral reefs across the Indo-Pacific. Renowned for its brilliant blue body, yellow tail, and black "palette" design, this species adds striking color and dynamic movement to any sufficiently sized marine aquarium.

These tangs are herbivorous, primarily grazing on algae, which makes them excellent natural cleaners for your tank. They require plenty of swimming space and thrive in well-established reef aquariums with live rock for grazing.

Specifications

Species: Paracanthurus hepatus
Size: Up to 12 inches
Temperament: Semi-aggressive, territorial
Diet: Herbivore (algae, spirulina)
Minimum Tank Size: 100 gallons
Reef Compatible: Yes, with caution

Care Instructions

Acclimation

Use drip acclimation method over 1-2 hours to gradually adjust to your tank's water parameters.

Diet & Feeding

Feed algae-based foods, spirulina, and marine vegetables 2-3 times daily.

Tank Environment

Requires ample swimming space, live rock for grazing, and stable water conditions.

Compatibility

Can be kept with other peaceful to semi-aggressive fish, but may fight with other tangs.

Health & Disease

Prone to ich and marine velvet. Maintain excellent water quality and quarantine new additions.
